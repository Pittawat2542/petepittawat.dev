# Content Cover Image System

This site uses content-specific raster cover images for blog posts, projects, publications, and talks. The visual language should stay consistent with the dark editorial reference cards: deep navy/indigo background, glassy depth, subtle technical grid, neon violet/blue highlights, and one clear symbolic subject that reflects the content.

## Canonical Style Prompt

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: website editorial cover image, 1200x630 landscape
Style/medium: polished cinematic tech editorial illustration, dark glassmorphism, subtle scientific diagram language, premium research-lab interface aesthetic
Scene/backdrop: deep navy-to-indigo environment with faint perspective grid, fine network lines, translucent panels, soft bloom, restrained particle details
Composition/framing: one strong symbolic subject centered or slightly off-center, crop-safe for card, article hero, and social preview usage; leave usable negative space near edges; no title text
Lighting/mood: calm, intelligent, reflective, low-key studio lighting with violet, blue, and occasional cyan accents
Color palette: near-black navy, indigo, violet, electric blue, soft cyan; avoid warm stock-photo palettes unless the topic demands a small accent
Materials/textures: frosted glass, luminous data lines, holographic panels, soft gradients, precise geometric overlays
Constraints: visually specific to the content, no generic abstract wallpaper, no in-image title, no UI mockup text, no watermark
Avoid: readable words, random letters, logos, fake app screens, photorealistic stock-photo scenes, cluttered diagrams, low-contrast muddy colors

## Per-Asset Prompt Template

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: `<blog | project | publication | talk>` cover image, 1200x630 landscape
Primary request: create a content-specific cover image for `<title>`.
Content signal: `<one- to three-sentence summary from excerpt, abstract, project summary, talk audience/resources, or first article headings>`.
Subject: `<one symbolic visual metaphor that represents the content>`.
Scene/backdrop: deep editorial technology space with glass panels, subtle grid, and quiet data/network texture.
Style/medium: cinematic dark tech editorial illustration matching the site-wide cover system.
Composition/framing: crop-safe landscape, strong focal subject, no text, readable at card thumbnail size.
Lighting/mood: reflective, precise, high-craft, soft violet/blue glow.
Color palette: navy, indigo, violet, blue, cyan, with only restrained secondary accents when content-specific.
Constraints: no title text, no watermark, no logos unless explicitly part of the content identity.
Avoid: generic AI brain imagery unless the content is specifically about identity/cognition; avoid literal screenshots unless the content is about an interface.

## Asset Paths

- Blog covers: `src/content/blog/<translationId>/cover.png`
- Blog frontmatter: English and Thai translations should both point to `../<translationId>/cover.png`
- Blog OG outputs: generated by `scripts/generate-og.ts` into `public/og/blog/{en,th}/<routeSlug>.png`
- Project cards: `public/visual/cards/projects/<id>.png`
- Publication cards: `public/visual/cards/publications/<id>.png`
- Talk cards: `public/visual/cards/talks/<id>.png`

## Review Checklist

- The image clearly reflects the specific content, not only its broad category.
- The image still belongs to the same site-wide visual family as the other covers.
- The image contains no readable title text, random words, watermark, or accidental logo.
- The focal subject remains legible at listing-card size.
- The image works under dark overlays in blog cards and article heroes.
- The final public card and OG outputs are `1200x630`.
